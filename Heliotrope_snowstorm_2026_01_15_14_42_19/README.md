# PROJECT TITLE
## Short Description
*An interactive system where a digital creature grows and changes through user-input text, visualising identity as a process of continuous accumulation.*
## Introduction
*The project explores how, within systems shaped by platforms and algorithms, individuals gradually take form through continuous input and feedback. In this context, identity is not seen as something fixed or inherent, but as something that is continuously shaped by external information.*
## Concept and Background Research
*The initial concept of this project is inspired by the idea of imitation. Imitation is often
understood as a way of adapting to new environments.*
*On a social level, individual identity is also influenced by living environments and social
structures. Sociologist Charles Cooley’s theory of the “Looking-Glass Self” suggests that self-understanding is gradually formed through the reactions of others and social feedback. People often establish and confirm their identity by imitating socially recognised language, behaviours, and modes of expression. Identity, in this sense, is not fixed, but is continuously shaped through interaction and feedback. This line of thinking further led me to focus on identity construction within digital systems.
Research on identity in digital space suggests that identity is not inherent, but is produced through interaction, media mechanisms, and persistent digital traces (Deh & Glođović, 2018). In platform-based environments, individuals continuously input information, which is recorded, processed, and fed back by systems in various forms.
Based on this perspective, I designed the project as a creature whose body is entirely constructed through user input, allowing the process of identity construction to be visualised through system behaviour and changing form.*
## How to Run / Install
*Open it in p5js or vscode.*
*Input text to feed the creature.*
*Each time it eats a food “text”, the creature grows a new tentacle made from that text. As more inputs are given, the tentacles accumulate, and the creature’s body becomes increasingly complex.*
